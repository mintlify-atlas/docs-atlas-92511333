The Part-Vehicle Relationships feature enables you to create associations between parts and the vehicles they're compatible with. This many-to-many relationship system allows a single part to be linked to multiple vehicles, and a single vehicle to be associated with many parts.

## Overview

The relationship management system provides:

- Many-to-many associations between parts and vehicles
- Visual display of linked vehicles for each part
- Easy linking and unlinking of vehicle associations
- Improved searchability and catalog organization

## Understanding Many-to-Many Relationships

The system uses a many-to-many relationship model:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="One Part, Multiple Vehicles" icon="car">
    A single part (like a universal oil filter) can be compatible with multiple vehicles (Fusca, Golf, Civic, etc.).
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="One Vehicle, Multiple Parts" icon="gear">
    A single vehicle can have many compatible parts associated with it in your catalog.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

This flexibility allows you to accurately represent real-world compatibility scenarios where parts may fit multiple vehicle models.

## Viewing Vehicle Associations

To see which vehicles are linked to a part:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select a Part">
    From the parts list, click on the part you want to view.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Access Part Details">
    Click the "Alterar" (Update) link to open the part edit view.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="View Relationships">
    Scroll down to the "Relacionamento com veículos" (Relationship with vehicles) section to see all linked vehicles.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The relationship section displays:
- A list of currently associated vehicles
- An option to add new vehicle associations
- The ability to remove existing associations

## Linking Parts to Vehicles

To create a new part-vehicle association:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Part Edit View">
    Navigate to the part details and click "Alterar" (Update) to enter edit mode.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select a Vehicle">
    In the "Relacionamento com veículos" section, use the dropdown menu to select a vehicle from the list of available vehicles.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create Association">
    Click the "Relacionar veículo" (Link vehicle) button to create the association.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm Success">
    The vehicle will immediately appear in the list of related vehicles for that part.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  The dropdown only shows vehicles that exist in your vehicles database. Make sure to create vehicles first if they don't appear in the list.
</Info>

## Removing Vehicle Associations

To unlink a vehicle from a part:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Locate the Association">
    In the part edit view, find the vehicle you want to unlink in the "Relacionamento com veículos" list.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Unlink">
    Click the "Desvincular" (Unlink) button next to the vehicle name.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm Removal">
    The vehicle will be removed from the associated vehicles list immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Unlinking a vehicle from a part doesn't delete the vehicle from your database - it only removes the association between that specific part and vehicle.
</Note>

## Use Cases and Benefits

Part-vehicle relationships provide significant value across multiple business scenarios:

### Improved Search Functionality

Customers can search for parts by vehicle name, making it easy to find all compatible parts for their specific vehicle model. This is especially useful when:

- A customer knows their vehicle but not the specific part name
- You want to display all available parts for a particular vehicle
- Creating vehicle-specific catalogs or recommendations

### Better Inventory Organization

Relationships help organize your inventory logically:

- Group parts by vehicle compatibility
- Identify gaps in your catalog (vehicles with few associated parts)
- Plan inventory based on popular vehicle models

### Enhanced Customer Experience

## Complete Workflow Example

Here's a complete workflow for managing part-vehicle relationships:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set Up Vehicles">
    First, create all the vehicles in your database. For example:
    - Fusca
    - Golf GTI  
    - Civic
    - Corolla
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create the Part">
    Add a new part to your catalog with all required details:
    - Name: "Universal Oil Filter"
    - Net Weight: 0.35
    - Gross Weight: 0.42
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Link Compatible Vehicles">
    Open the part for editing and link all compatible vehicles:
    - Select "Fusca" from dropdown → Click "Relacionar veículo"
    - Select "Golf GTI" from dropdown → Click "Relacionar veículo"
    - Select "Civic" from dropdown → Click "Relacionar veículo"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Verify Associations">
    Confirm that all three vehicles now appear in the related vehicles list for the Universal Oil Filter.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Test Search Functionality">
    Go to the parts list and search for "Civic" in the vehicle search field. The Universal Oil Filter should appear in the results.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Build Vehicle Database First">
    Create your complete vehicle database before starting to link parts. This prevents interruptions in your workflow and ensures all vehicles are available when you need them.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Verify Compatibility">
    Always verify actual vehicle compatibility before creating associations. Incorrect relationships can lead to customer dissatisfaction and returns.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Regular Audits">
    Periodically review part-vehicle relationships to ensure they remain accurate, especially when:
    - New vehicle models are released
    - Part specifications change
    - You receive customer feedback about compatibility
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Batch Processing">
    When adding multiple parts that share the same vehicle compatibility (e.g., a new line of filters), document the common vehicles to streamline the linking process.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Document Exceptions">
    If a part is compatible with most versions of a vehicle but not all (e.g., "Golf GTI 2015+ only"), include this information in the part's application vehicle field for clarity.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## API Integration

The relationship system is supported by dedicated API endpoints:

| Endpoint | Method | Purpose |
|----------|--------|----------|
| `/parts/:partId/vehicles` | GET | List all vehicles related to a part |
| `/parts/:partId/vehicles/:vehicleId` | POST | Link a vehicle to a part |
| `/parts/:partId/vehicles/:vehicleId` | DELETE | Remove vehicle-part relationship |

These endpoints enable integration with external systems and automation of relationship management.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Vehicle Not Appearing in Dropdown">
    If a vehicle doesn't appear in the selection dropdown:
    - Verify the vehicle exists in your vehicles database
    - Refresh the page to ensure the latest vehicle data is loaded
    - Check that the vehicle hasn't been deleted
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Cannot Link Vehicle">
    If you cannot create a vehicle association:
    - Ensure you've selected a vehicle from the dropdown
    - Check that the same vehicle isn't already linked to the part
    - Verify your connection to the backend API
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Search Not Returning Expected Results">
    If searching by vehicle doesn't show a part:
    - Verify the part-vehicle relationship exists in the database
    - Check your search term spelling
    - Remember that the search performs partial matching
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
